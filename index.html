Message to new Sapnights

Welcome to Sapna, here you'll be able to polish your skills as a developer while collaboraing with like-minded individuals at the University of Technology, Jamaica. Everyone at Sapna brings different skills to the table and we pool them together in order to create amazing software. While you as a new Sapnight are not expected to know everything, we will not hand hold you in the learning process, you must learn for yourself. We will guide you along the way and you shall guide us like iron sharpens iron. At the end you will become a valuable developer in whatever area you choose to specialize in and have a grate time getting there.

What is Sapna?

SAPNA is an initiative born out of the School of Computing and Information Technology (SCIT) at the University of Technology, Jamaica. The aim of the initiative is to enhance the knowledge and technical skills of its student members through participation in enterprise software application development and deployment. SAPNA was inspired by a dream or desire for SCIT to produce an excellent caliber of graduates who would be dominant on the world stage in their abilities to perform at a very high level on any software development project in anywhere in the world.

How to Contribute

Sapna manages it's project using Git Repositories, predominantly GitLab. As such, knowledge of using the Git version management tool is mandatory. For an introduction to Git, click this link In order to contribute to one one these projects you have to contact the project manager in charge of that project, you can find them in the details of the project. Afterwards you will either be in charge of implementing a feature or you will be able to view the 'issues' of the project; issues are 'free-for-all' problems that anyone can be in charge of solving. What you will be doing depends on your skill level. As you get better you can tackle bigger problems and even collaborate with other Sapnights in order to complete the goals of the project. Each Project requires different skills in order to complete, the project will clearly state what skills will be needed in order to complete the project. Most of the projects are web development based so HTML , CSS ,and Javascript will be required. That is all you need to know, check out on one of the Active Projects below to get started. ...
